Legends of UK

King Arthur

The Legend of King Arthur and Merlin. One of the most famous British legends tells of King Arthur, who, according to legend, united Britain and defended it from its enemies. He became king by pulling a sword from a stone—a symbol of his destiny. The wise wizard Merlin was always at his side, offering advice and magic. And in the legendary fortress of Camelot, the Knights of the Round Table gathered to uphold justice.

Loch Ness Monster

The Legend of the Loch Ness Monster. According to legend, a huge, mysterious creature resembling an ancient lizard with a long neck lives in the deep Loch Ness of Scotland. The first mentions of the monster appeared as early as the sixth century. Since then, many have claimed to have seen a silhouette or dark shadow in the water. Some consider this a myth, others believe it is proof of the existence of ancient animals that have survived to this day.

Robin Hood

The Legend of Robin Hood. Robin Hood is a legendary noble outlaw who lived in Sherwood Forest in England. According to legend, he and his loyal friends robbed the rich and helped the poor. His main enemy was the Sheriff of Nottingham, and his companion was the beautiful Lady Marian. Robin Hood became a symbol of justice and the fight against unjust authority.
